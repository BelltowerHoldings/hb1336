National context

Move-in costs in national context

The right comparison is not each state’s nominal security-deposit cap, but the total amount a landlord may legally collect up front — first month’s rent, last month’s rent, prepaid or advance rent, and the security deposit, combined. By that measure New Hampshire is among the most restrictive states in the country, even where another state’s deposit cap alone looks similar.

A one-month deposit cap can still sit beside a much larger move-in bill. Use the toggle to switch between the two views: by security deposit alone, several states look as strict as New Hampshire — but by total move-in funds, many of those same states (Massachusetts and California among them) separately permit last month’s rent or prepaid rent on top, so the cash a renter must actually produce exceeds New Hampshire’s. New Hampshire is the baseline in both views.
